When: Nov. 2020 - Dec. 2020 (1 month)

Class: Information & Grid Design

Team: Me, Myself, and I

Tech: Adobe XD, Photoshop

Focus: Interaction Design, Modular Grid Design

Purpose

Design excellence is too often measured by a few names and limited cultures. Therefore, the goal of the project is to use modular grid, words & images relationship, and interaction design to invite users to overcome selective exposure and learn more about less well-known female designers from different nationalities and cultures. I chose to showcase specifically female designers for my project because when I first searched for ‘greatest graphic designers’, I found that many sites only included male designers. Therefore, I want to use my project as a platform for people to learn more about talented women in design.

Research

After exploring various websites and google links, I narrowed my scope and focused by search on women designers from different countries who uses bold color in her work. Finally, I decided to include six designers in my project. They are Fanette Mellier, Lotta Nieminen, Jing Zhang, Susan Kare, Verena Michelitsch, and Vicki Turner.

I decided to showcase those designers for the reasons explained below:

  1. I love Fanette Mellier uses color to structure her graphic design projects and communicate with her audiences
  2. I’m inspired by Lotta Nieminen’s ability to work as both a graphic designer and an illustrator and and how she’s able to develop multiple visions and voices simultaneously
  3. I love how Jing Zhang is able to present a dense imformation in a playful manner without using a single word
  4. Susan Kare is one of the most iconic designer for graphical user interface and icons
  5. I’m impressed by how Verena Michelitsch is able to balance the interaction between geometric shapes and bold colors in her illustrations
  6. I low how Vicki Turner is able to present everyday objects and nature in a colorful, unique, and clean style

Sketches

The project focused on a two-screen experience, in this case, the two-screen was the home page, and Jing Zhang (designer)’s page. I explored possible visual modular grid approaches and font combinations through several thumbnail sketches.

Final Design

For the home screen, I decided to create a background pattern with my personal favorite artwork from all the designers of my choice. Since all of the artworks re very colorful, to help balance the presence of the title and images on my home screen, I photoshopped all the artworks to black and white, then I added an interaction feature such that when users hover over a specific artwork, that artwork will turn from black and white to color. I believe this will intrigue readers and encourage them to hover over individual artworks and thus invite them to examine those artworks individually and in detail. The designer I chose to design the structure and content interaction for is Jing Zhang. I love how she brings information to life with her design and the number of details she put in her work. To complement her artworks and her style (3D-like), I chose to implement an interaction feature that enlarges an artwork once the user hovers over it. I believe this interaction feature enhances the 3D-ness in Zhang’s work and brings her works to life.

Prototype

Unfortunately, hotspot hints are not available with this prototype. Below are some helpful hints to help you navigate the prototype.

  1. Main Screen: Click on specific artworks (side view of a person’s head) to navigate to the second screen
  2. Designer Screen: Hover over artworks for interaction

Reflection

This project allowed me to focus on interaction design and how to invite the user to tap, drag, and animate to experience some surprise at the next level that invites further looking. Moreover, I got to explore with modular grid and use that to create opportunities for comparison and selection. While working on the project, I learned that my first design is not going to be my final product, and that’s okay. There needs to be enough time to explore, try things out, mess things up, and try again. That’s how I can produce my best works. Moreover, I learned that whenever I’m stuck or out of ideas, it’s okay to step away from the desk to get new ideas and perspectives to the work. Lastly, I love how I was able to use design to address a psychology practice (selective exposure).
