Purpose

Adam Causgrove, the Associate Director of Corporate Relations for CMU, wants a platform for the Dietrich College to catalog the many different ways external partners can work with faculty and students. The purpose of this website is to showcase the many different ways external partners can work with faculty and students at Dietrich and to bridge the gap between Dietrich and external partners.

Challenge

Although similar sites exist for other schools or centers at CMU, such site currently doesn’t exist for Dietrich College. Therefore, extensive research was needed to understand my client’s goal and what information needs to be included in the site. Moreover, the constraint for the web design was that the layout of the site needs to conform to standard CMU communication policies.

Competitive Analysis

We looked at existing websites from other colleges at CMU as well as other universities. This includes CMU’s Heinz College and Drexel University. Since such websites need to present a lot of information, we found that some platforms such as Drexel University’s website tend to be too text heavy, which can discourage users from exploring the site. This problem, however, is nicely addressed in Heinz website, where they utilize landing pictures and various interaction designs to make the site more visually appealing and interesting to explore. Moreover, in Heinz College Website, links are made with a purpose: everything is aimed at external partners getting access to connecting with students or faculty.

User Research

During our first meeting with our client Adam, the associate director of corporate relations of Dietrich college, we understood that the main goal of the website is to advertise opportunities to engage with Dietrich. Based on that, we identified three user groups: external partners, CMU faculty and CMU staff. To better understand each group’s pain point and what their expectations are for the site, we conducted three preliminary interviews with representatives from our user groups.

After synthesizing our interviews results, we found several notable pain points:

  1. Currently, most of the collaboration was established via personal connection/professional conference. There lacks a channel to find information about potential external partners
  2. It is hard to find contact information about external partners therefore failed to reach out to them
  3. Many staffs currently use a google spreadsheet to keep track of external partners’ information and reach out process, which can get messy

Our interviewees also expressed their ideal vision on connecting with partners, and we’d like to narrow our focus down to these goals:

  1. Having a way to help faculty connect with CMU alumni and non-profit organizations around Pittsburgh area
  2. Showcasing projects and examples with executive summary & details so that it will be easier for staff to explain the projects to potential collaborators
  3. Provide an option to tag and filter projects by categories, and also provide links to relevant resources such as past collaborations and contact information

Persona

From our user research and preliminary interviews, we came out with three different personas:

  1. External partner who wants to find the right candidate to help them with a specific business need such as new database implementation
  2. CMU faculty who wants to effectively connect with potential clients for a class they are teaching or for a research project
  3. CMU staff who wants to promote CMU to external partners and attract more professional opportunities for students and faculty

Sitemap

With our user interviews and persona pain points in mind, we constructed a sitemap to document our information architecture and site organization.

Low-Fi Wireframes
& Usability Testing

From our sitemap, we chose to wireframe and implement the landing page and student projects page since it appears that users care the most about those two pages based on our user research. In our low-fi version, we utilized various pictures with succinct descriptions to present information. Since the target audience for the site is working professionals, we believed that a desktop-first approach is appropriate for the project.

Mid-Fi Wireframes
& Usability Testing

For our mid-fi wireframes, we added a CMU ranking section in our landing page as we felt like it could be a great advertising message to attract more partners. For our student projects page, we decided to utilize carousels to present different projects and enable users to filter by program or by semester.

Final Design

Based on the comment we received, we made further editing to our wireframes. In our final version, we highlighted staff contact information on the very top so that external partners can have easy access to it. We also took out the ranking section and added a section highlighting different ways an external partner can engage with Dietrich college. For the student page, we created a carousel on top showcasing past highlights, and added another navigation bar to allow users to quickly navigate through different departments.

Transition Design

We choose mostly fluid as our transition strategy because that’s the transition strategy that CMU uses for the Dietrich College website and we want to follow the convention.
